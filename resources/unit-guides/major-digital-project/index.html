Your Assignment

In developing a major project proposal, you will need to consider your experience of, and knowledge gathered in, your studies sofar, and your future aims as a designer. You should be ambitious in defining your areas of research and practice.

You may continue from your previous work in the Digital Design Portfolio, draw particular threads from it, or begin a new project entirely.This is an opportunity to position yourself as a practitioner within the world of design. Consider what kind of research you will undertake, the type of work you want to be making, how you will make it, and what your aims and intentions are in releasing this into the world.

This needs to be a project you can complete to a high standard within the time available. This project will usually be presented at agraduate showcase butdoes not necessarily need to be ready for showcase presentation. Your work mayexist in multiple different contextsand there will be a short period of time after the hand in to prepare it for the showcase and your portfolio.

Submissions should include research and process reflection and must also contain comprehensive documentation of a completed, designed and functional project.

Learning Outcomes

The unit learning outcomes and how they can be evidenced
On completion of this unit you will be able to: You will show evidence of this by:
Demonstrate expertise in engaging in sustained, in-depth and relevant researchfrom diverse sources (Enquiry) Your research will show an ability to conduct mixed methods in depth research into your chosen topic. The research will be clearly and visually presented and show how you have drawn on a variety of sources.
Exhibit expertise in applying critical and analytical skills and synthesis of ideasfrom research materials in the realisation of project work (Enquiry) Your project will show how you have critically synthesised the research to inform your design.
Demonstrate competence and confidence in applying expert subject knowledgeto a final portfolio of work (Knowledge) You will demonstrate awareness of the field and of your position within it, showing how you have understood the work of key designers active in your particular project field
Identify, thoroughly explore, develop and process appropriate media, materialand techniques relevant to projects while considering the social effects andenvironmental impact of these decisions.(Process) Your project will show a range of experiments using different media, interactions, technologies, and modes of experience. These will be appropriate to the project and show a consideration for their wider impact.
Fully showcase substantial technical and craft skills (Realisation) The quality of design finish will be exceptional, and the level of technical implementation at professional standard.
Demonstrate a high level of expertise in selecting and applying appropriatecommunication, design and presentation models (Communication) You will show that you have made considered design choices around format, tone and application that are appropriate for your audience or context.
Confidently select and describe strategies for managing projects, deadlines andconsultation with appropriate sources (Process) You will make your thinking visible through outcomes that are part of the work in progress development of your project.
Demonstrate and apply knowledge of the appropriate professional context forthe work (Process) You will show how you have managed and scheduled your time on the project, evidencing each stage of the design development through documentation.

Assessment Information

Assessment requirements

Portfolio of work: documenting the outcomes for the set project work. This will also include a process blog and/or sketchbook documenting your iterative design and development process specifically in the areas of graphic design and information visualisation.

This unit is assessed holistically (100% of the unit). Assessment will be against the specified marking criteria, the UAL site has assessment guides, policy and regulations
