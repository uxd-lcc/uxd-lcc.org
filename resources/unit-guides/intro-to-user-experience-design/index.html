Brief: Drawing Systems

Design and implement/run a rule-based system which generates a series of images.

You (the Designer) will design and write, or otherwise record, a set of rules that describe a system of image generation. This will then be run by another member of the class (the Implementor) who will give you the images. You'll need to consider how you use design to communicate your intentions to the Implementor, you might need to write a series of rules, provide diagrams or instructions, create custom tools or software for them to use.

This process the Implementor follows should produce at least 20 images, which you will edit down to a set of 10 to present alongside the rules and tools used.

Context

Images or forms can be created or designed directly: we can place pixels, draw lines or shape lumps of clay. They can also be designed in a less direct way, by creating a set of rules for how someone or something else might create or design them.

The American artist Sol LeWitt is well known for his large scale wall drawings. LeWitt didn’t draw these himself, but sent instructions to the galleries in which they were exhibited to allow a third party to produce them. For LeWitt, the artistic process was in the planning and decisions that went into these instructions, rather than in the execution of the drawing, saying that “the idea becomes the machine that makes the art”.

Today, many designers and artists write code - instructions for a computer - as part of their practice, using computation as a medium to generate images and forms. This project will introduce you to that process.

When writing your instructions, consider the following:

How much control do you want over the outcome? How much chance will you allow to enter your system?

Is there a particular place, space, time, or mindset the Implementor will need to be in to follow your instructions?

What does the Implementor need to understand in order to follow your rules? Do you need to give them any special tools? How can you make use of their particular skills or knowledges?

Learning Outcomes

The unit learning outcomes and how they can be evidenced
On completion of this unit you will be able to: You will show evidence of this by:
Demonstrate and apply knowledge of the appropriate professional context for the work. (Process) You will explore and reflect on your own and others’ cultural capital in the context of User Experience Design as a global discipline, by means of surveying, drawing, photographing, reading, writing and referencing material from primary and secondary sources and evidence this in your presentation.
Evidence of skills needed for effective learning and communication of ideas, arguments and criticism in visual and written forms (Communication) You will present your ideas and arguments in visual, verbal, written and oral modes that evidence your ability to visualise, read, research and reference.

You will analyse images, objects and texts through careful visual and written examination, and formulate an argument or position in relation to material that you have selected to visualise, debate or write about.
Evidence of understanding of the importance of studentship through engagement and participation with the course and your student colleagues (Enquiry) You will record the process of critically engaging with the unit, staff and peers, by attending workshops, studio sessions, lectures, seminars and visits, and visually and verbally communicate this in your presentation.
Evidence of your engagement with the principles of critical reflection and a commitment to personal and professional development (Process) Keep a record of your thinking and doing through a process blog. This should show critical reflection, working with your peers and documentation of your learning achievements. It should be summarised in your presentation.

Assessment Information

Assessment requirements

This unit is assessed on a pass/fail basis. You will receive written feedback but no grade will be assigned to your work.

You will be assessed on your presentation covering both your CTS and Studio Work. This unit is assessed holistically (100% of the unit). Assessment will be against the specified marking criteria, the UAL site has assessment guides, policy and regulations
