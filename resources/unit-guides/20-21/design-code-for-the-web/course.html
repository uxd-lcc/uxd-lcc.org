Course Tutorials

Tutorials

Tutorials are one to one or small group meetings to discuss your work and progress, requiring you to prepare and contribute as well as receive feedback. Individual (one to one) tutorials are also a chance for you to discuss non-academic concerns you may have which may be impacting your work.

Some tutorials may be focused on a specific matter such as preparing for a study choice or discussing your feedback following assessment. Your tutor will listen and respond to your needs and questions in a tutorial but will also alert you to any issues regarding your progress, and provide constructive advice.

Timetabled tutorials will vary in length and regularity depending on the stage you are at, and your individual needs. Often, a one to one tutorial is 15-20 minutes but a group tutorial (up to eight students) may be an hour. We offer a minimum of 1 formal tutorial per term (usually around assessment points), and you will find that a lot of the studio time will give you an opportunity to discuss your work and progress including informal project tutorials when you need them.

You will need to sign up for your timetabled tutorials to select a time this will be done via an online form. The tutorial is a dialogue: whether you are in a one to one or a group, it is important both to listen and to contribute, being respectful of othersâ€™ views. You should make notes (or record your tutorial, subject to permissions) to help you reflect on the discussion and your learning. If there is anything you have not understood, contact your tutor.

Location

Most tutorials will be held online, using Collaborate Ultra, but can involve other software if it helps the disussion.

Support & Advice

Academic Support

Theresa Wilkie, Head of Academic Support
t.wilkie@lcc.arts.ac.uk

Academic support is available to all students at all levels of study. If you would like to enhance your academic skills such as planning and structuring essays, referencing and plagiarism, critical thinking, independent learning, reading effectively or preparing for lectures, you may find Academic Support a useful resource. Workshops, one to one tutorials and online advice are available.

Language Support

Information on moodle or email language-centre@arts.ac.uk

If you are not a native English speaker, you may benefit from the support offered by the Language Centre . It runs free classes, workshops and one-to-one tutorials. Staff in the Centre can help you with the academic language and skills you need to be successful in your studies.

Disability Services

Information on moodle or email disability@arts.ac.uk

Disability services are available if you are disabled, have learning difficulties or are experiencing long-term health or mental health issues. They provide specific support and information to help you achieve the best from your learning experience. All information is confidential.
