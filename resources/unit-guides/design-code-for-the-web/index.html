Your Assignment

You will design and build artefacts that are part of the network known as the Internet. They may consume from, publish to or simply be situated online.

You will gain an understanding of the material of the internet, such as interfaces, code, data, and infrastructures, which mediate many of our interactions today. We will evaluate the role technologies, platforms and design play and how they hold significant social and political power. As informed designers we need to understand the medium we design for as well as using practical methods to design and build for it.

You will be introduced to web technologies and practical methods for designing and building interfaces, working with data and creating dynamic online experiences. You will study and deploy methods from information design, employ best practices from interface design and regularly consider the user or audience experience. Your experimentation, creative process and learning journey should be reflected upon personally, through documentation and presentations of your concepts.

Brief 01: Interactive Hypertext Narrative

Create an interactive hypertext narrative that reveals something about yourself or an experience you've had. It could be a story about yourself, a journey through a topic you're interested in, a mode of navigation that mimics your personality, etc. There should be multiple HTML pages, linked together by </a> tags. Each page could contain text and/or images. No styling. Limit your focus to story and interaction.

Brief originally devised by Lauren McCarthy for DESMA:Network Media course.

Brief 03: Community Interface

Create a homepage for a community. It can be for a community that you're involved with, one that you admire, or a fictional community. For instance, a site for a colony of robots that live on the moon, a nonprofit organisation of your choice, or a site for a club or student group that you're a member of or would like to start. You can imagine that this homepage is part of a larger website, but the links don't needed to lead anywhere. You can write <a href="#">my link</a> to make a link that goes nowhere.

You can try to recreate layout and interface patterns you've seen online, or be more experimental with your design. In any case, your design should be clearly informed by the content it is representing. Ask yourself, how can you use web design to communicate an idea, identity, and community?

Your design does not need to be responsive, but should work on either a standard laptop monitor or a mobile phone (your choice). When you post your project link, also post a screenshot as it looks on your device for reference.

Learning Outcomes

The unit learning outcomes and how they can be evidenced
On completion of this unit you will be able to: You will show evidence of this by:
Demonstrate an awareness of working in a digital, design-led context toward a specified outcome using appropriate processes and methods to record research and the development of ideas and concepts. (Enquiry) Through your design outcomes, blog posts and reflective writing you will show evidence of your research. You will be able to explain how you developed an understanding of a topic and evidence the resources you used.
Demonstrate the ability to use visual research and online resources to inform project outcomes. (Enquiry) Influences from relevant art and design practices will guide your design decisions and be evident in your outcomes. These will also be discussed during crits, tutorials and documented in your final submission.
Demonstrate an awareness of fundamental principles of design and coding for the web whilst considering its capacity for increasing or decreasing privacy, openness and inclusion. (Knowledge) Your outcomes and documented experiments will demonstrate competencies in design and coding for the web, such as information hierarchies, typographic principles, and interface design standards.
Experiment with a variety of digital processes to enhance meaning and communication. (Process) Evidence of idea development will be shown through practical outcomes and documentation of sketches, prototypes, and experiments with technologies and/or design concepts.
Present digital solutions to peers and articulate your visual thinking. (Communication) Through your final submission you will discuss the evolution of your ideas and your learning as a direct result of feedback from presenting work to peers and tutors.
Demonstrate an ability to engage with directed digital production and manage your time (Process, Realisation) You will show how you have taken risks in your learning through use of new materials and methods whilst also demonstrating an awareness of practical project concerns such as time management.
Demonstrate an awareness of the fundamental capacity of the internet to effect privacy, openness and inclusion. (Knowledge) Class discussions and workshops will relate directly to the potential negative consequences of the technology and media we study. Your engagement in this subject will be evidenced through your designed outcomes or through reflective writing in your submission.

Assessment Information

Assessment requirements

You should submit a PDF documenting the outcomes for the set project work. Linked from the first page will be a research weblog and/or sketchbook documenting your iterative design and development process specifically in the areas of graphic design, animation and coding for the web.

This unit is assessed holistically (100% of the unit). Assessment will be against the specified marking criteria below. You will receive written feedback, as well as a final grade for your submission. The UAL site has assessment guides, policy and regulations.
